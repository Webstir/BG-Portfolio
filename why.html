So often in the world we encounter problems that are framed in a a 'us vs. them' mindset. In other words, for one group to prosper, the other must suffer. This kind of framing is short-sighted and it forces us to take sides and stay stuck in our way of looking at the problem. Design thinking offers a much needed antidote. By hearing and understanding the needs of all groups, we can craft non-zero sum win-win solutions.


When approaching any problem, it's tempting to jump right into the implementation, the visual design, and the polish. However, you risk missing the forest for the trees. To find novel solutions and approaches, it's often helpful to zoom out, reexamine your underlying assumptions, and see if the problem can be approached from a different angle.


Behind every seemingly simple answer, there's always another perspective, nuance, or lens to be uncovered. While you can't eliminate your assumptions, you can start testing and unpacking each one. Conceptual, big-picture UX thinking casts a wide net to find the right solution.
