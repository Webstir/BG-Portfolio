Faced with the weight of this global, life-threatening disaster, I had to do something.

Credit: HowStuffWorks

At UC Davis, I learned the latest thinking in sustainable design.

Urban and suburban sprawl chokes off the biosphere and leads to a lot of wasted energy and land. I wrote an essay about Arcologies principles and how they can provide a sustainable model for the future.

As I worked on various projects, I realized the constraints of a site were ultimately determined by urban planning requirements. As long as we live in a culture dominated by cars, my designs were ultimately constrained by that need.

Only by first having a transportation paradigm shift could we truly be sustainable.

In 2010, I learned of the emergent technology of electric bicycles and started working for an eBike start-up.

eBikes are the fastest, cheapest, most sustainable, most fun way to get around and they don't contribute to congestion.

What more do you want?

At City CarShare, I managed our ~50 electic carsharing fleet. In partnership with Toyota, we had a prototype program of 30 IQ-EVs, a 2-seater all-electric car—perfect for urban environments.

I also lead and designed an electric bikesharing program, which would have been one the first in the country.

What can you do?

eBike, bike, eScoot, eSkate, walk and take public transit.

Participate in the sharing economy.

Reduce food waste. Switch to a plant-based diet, or at least eat less beef.

Repair, not replace. Buy second-hand goods.

Fly less. When you do fly, buy carbon offsets.

Switch to solar. If you're a renter, consider PG&E's Solar Choice program.

Donate to The Ocean Cleanup project.

Protect our rainforest.

Let's tackle wicked problems.
